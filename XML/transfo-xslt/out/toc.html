Noms de personnes

Nom Autres noms Note
Aulë

    Aulë was a Vala and one of the Aratar, also known as the Smith and Smith of the Valar, concerned with rock, metal, nature of substances and works of craft.
    Irmo
    • Lorien

    Irmo, commonly called Lórien after his place of dwelling, was a Vala who was known as "the master of visions and dreams."
    Mandos

      Mandos was the Doomsman of the Valar who pronounced judgement in matters of fate. He was the keeper of the slain in his Halls in the west of Valinor.

      His actual, less common name was Namo. He was the "elder brother" of the Vala Irmo. He is one of the Aratar. Together, Mandos and Irmo are called the Fëanturi ("Masters of Spirits).Their sister is Nienna. Vairë the Weaver is his wife.
      Nienna

        Nienna was a Queen of the Valar, the sister of Mandos and Irmo, acquainted with grief and sorrow but also pity and courage.
        Vana

          Vána, one of the Valier, was the younger sister of Yavanna and the spouse of Oromë. Among the seven Valier, Vána was the sixth named.
          Yavanna
          • Palurien
          • Kémi
          • Dame de la Terre

          Yavanna Kementári was a Valië, the Queen of Earth. Also known as Giver of Fruits, she was responsible for all growing things.

          Yavanna's surname was Palúrien ("bosom of earth"), a Qenya name, at an earlier stage of writing of The Silmarillion. Kementári first appeared as Kémi from root KEME (Gnomish Címir).

          Ent
          • Shepherds of the Trees

          The Ents were sentient, humanoid beings created at the request of Yavanna to protect the trees from other creatures, particularly Dwarves,and thus were called "Shepherds of the Trees".
          Valar
          • Gods

          The Valar (sg. Vala) were the Powers of Arda who shaped and ruled the world. They lived on the Western continent of Aman.

          The Gods is a name wrongly given by Men to the Valar. When the first Edain newly arrived in Beleriand and disputed whether or not to remain, they referred to the Valar as gods in the West.They had not at this point been tutored by the Elves in such high matters.

          Noms de Lieux

          Nom Autres noms Note
          Aman

            Aman, the Blessed Realm, was a continent that lay to the west of Middle-earth, across the great ocean Belegaer. It contained Valinor, the home of the Valar, and Eldamar, the kingdom of the Calaquendi.
            Cercle du Destin
            • Máhanaxar

            Máhanaxar, also called Rithil-Anamo, was the Ring of Doom, probably a hall or court where the Valar held their councils.
            Kulullin

              Kulullin and Silindrin were two cauldrons according to the early version of the legendarium in The Book of Lost Tales.
              Laurelin
              • Lindelokse
              • Golden Tree
              • Malinalda
              • Culurien

              Laurelin was the Golden Tree of Aman, the female mate of Telperion, and the younger of the Two Trees of Valinor. Laurelin had gold-trimmed leaves and her dew was collected by Varda in her Wells.
              Silindrin

                Kulullin and Silindrin were two cauldrons according to the early version of the legendarium in The Book of Lost Tales.
                Telperion
                • Silpion
                • Ninquelote

                Telperion was the elder of the Two Trees of Valinor, called the White Tree, which shed silver light on the domain of the Valar. His leaves were of dark green, shining silver beneath, and his boughs were decked with brilliant flowers that shed a rain of silver dew, which was collected as a source of water and of light.
                Valinor

                  Valinor or Valinórë was the realm of the Valar in central Aman. Valinor was the place to which the Valar moved after Almaren was destroyed by Melkor.

                  Valinor properly refers to the inhabited lands of the continent of Aman, home to the Valar and the three kindreds of Elves: the Vanyar, some of the Noldor, and some of the Teleri. Valimar is its central city.
